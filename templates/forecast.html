How to read this forecast

What this page is for. You get a forward view of revenue and booking volumes month by month for the next {{ horizon }} months (change the horizon with the buttons below to refocus KPIs, charts, and the table). Everything is benchmarked against AOP - the annual operating plan - so you can see whether the trajectory is ahead or behind target.

National, area, and practice. {% if level == 'national' %} National adds every practice in the network into one series. {% elif level == 'area' %} Area adds only the practices in {{ area.name }}. {% else %} Practice is a single site - {{ practice.name }}. {% endif %} Under the hood, area and national numbers are simply the sum of the same per-practice forecasts you would see by drilling down, so the story stays consistent at every level.

How the forecast is built. Each month applies UK seasonality (typical busy and quiet months from historical patterns). Revenue also reflects assumed price paths: NHS +2.5% per year and private +5% per year, blended by each practice’s NHS / private mix. When the LightGBM model is deployed, it predicts using recent booking and revenue history, mix, capacity, utilisation targets, booking profile, and related features, stepping forward one month at a time. If no trained model is present, a statistical baseline uses a de-seasonalised average of the last six completed months plus an adjustment for how strongly the practice is booking versus plan so far this year.

Forecast vs AOP. AOP revenue for each future month starts from plan levels, applies the same seasonal shape, includes a +3% uplift on the current-year plan, and moves with the same NHS/private price assumptions. Gap vs AOP is forecast revenue minus AOP revenue. Delivery % is forecast divided by AOP for that period. Colour cues match the rest of the app: green at about 97% of AOP or above, amber between 90% and 97%, and red below 90%.

The shaded 80% band on the revenue chart. This is an uncertainty range, not a “worst case / best case”. With the ML model, the band comes from trained lower and upper predictions (roughly 10th–90th percentile); with the statistical path, it is driven by how volatile that practice’s historical bookings have been. Wider bands mean more historical variability (or model uncertainty), not that something has gone wrong.

Charts. The main line chart compares forecast revenue (blue) to AOP revenue (green dashed). Markers are green or red depending whether that month is above or below AOP. The bar chart shows forecast booking counts - implied from revenue using blended fee rates and the same pricing uplift logic. The Forecast assumptions panel on the right restates the key inputs.

Monthly forecast detail table. For each month: expected bookings, forecast revenue, AOP revenue, monetary gap, delivery percentage, and the 80% CI revenue range (lower–upper). The footer row totals the horizon you selected.
